Some facts about summer:

1. Although calendar summer begins on June 1 and lasts 3 months, astronomical summer in the Northern Hemisphere lasts from June 20 (21) to September 22 (23), in the Southern Hemisphere - from December 21 (22) to March 20 (21).

2. It's the loudest time of the year. Thunderstorms are more common in summer than at any other time. The warmth of summer often provides the ideal conditions needed for a thunderstorm to form.

3. The longest day of the year is in summer. June 21 is the summer solstice.

4. On a hot day in Paris, the Eiffel Tower gets taller. The tower is built of iron and expands when heated, causing the structure to "grow" by as much as 17 centimeters.

5. In summer, crickets chirp, and the air temperature can be determined from the singing of a cricket. We count the number of chirps in 8 seconds and add 5 to this value - this way you can get the temperature in degrees Celsius. This is an absolutely scientific fact - the formula was derived by the physicist Amos Dolber.

6. The hottest summer temperature was recorded in the "Valley of Death" (a desert area in California) - 56.66 degrees Celsius.

7. At temperatures above 40 degrees Celsius, the human body loses about 1 liter of sweat per hour.
